The Challenges

The Gates Foundation faced a dual-audience communications challenge that required bridging two worlds:

1 Speak to policymakers and donors in language they trust—data-driven, evidence-based, and ROI-focused—to secure and sustain funding for PHC systems.
2 Reach rural and peri-urban communities with stories they feel—culturally authentic, emotionally resonant, and accessible across literacy levels—to drive actual uptake of PHC services. Adding to the complexity, PHC in Nigeria contends with: • Mistrust of public health systems • Cultural and religious resistance to topics like family planning and immunization • Misinformation spreading through community networks • Low visibility of PHC as a priority in national development conversations The question became: How do you make a systemic health infrastructure feel personal, trusted, and urgent—to both a minister in Abuja and a mother in a remote village?

Creative Approach

Our approach centered on "Precision Minimalist." We utilized a grid-heavy layout to emphasize order and reliability, while the color palette was chosen to reflect professional authority.
